Road Trips

  1. The Alpine Showcase (South Island): This journey is the quintessential South Island experience, beginning in the garden city of Christchurch and heading straight into the heart of the Southern Alps. As you drive toward Lake Tekapo, the landscape transforms into the vast, golden tussock of the Mackenzie Basin, where the impossibly turquoise waters of the glacial lakes serve as a foreground to the snow-capped peaks. A stay at Aoraki/Mt Cook National Park allows you to hike the Hooker Valley Track, crossing suspension bridges to reach the terminal lake of the Hooker Glacier. Continuing through the dramatic Lindis Pass, you’ll arrive in the lakeside town of Wanaka before finishing in Queenstown, the adventure capital of the world, where the Remarkables mountain range meets the crystal-clear shores of Lake Wakatipu.

  2. The Thermal Explorer (North Island): Starting in the cosmopolitan hub of Auckland, this route dives into the geothermal and cultural wonders of the North Island. Your first major stop is Waitomo, where you can drift through underground caves illuminated by thousands of tiny glowworms. Moving toward Rotorua, the air fills with the scent of sulfur as you encounter bubbling mud pools, erupting geysers, and the living Māori village of Ohinemutu. After exploring the massive expanse of Lake Taupō—a crater created by one of history's most powerful eruptions—the road leads you over the rugged hills to the coastal city of Napier. Here, you can spend your final days admiring the world-famous Art Deco architecture and enjoying some of the country’s finest Syrah and Chardonnay in the Hawke's Bay wine region.

  3. The Wild West Coast (South Island): This is a journey through a land that time forgot, characterized by ancient rainforests and a thunderous coastline. After crossing the Southern Alps via the spectacular Arthur’s Pass, you emerge on the West Coast, where the Tasman Sea crashes against limestone formations like the Punakaiki Pancake Rocks. The drive south takes you between the mountains and the sea, leading to the twin wonders of Franz Josef and Fox Glaciers, where massive rivers of ice descend into the temperate rainforest. The final leg takes you through the Haast Pass, a remote and mountainous stretch filled with hidden waterfalls and dense beech forests, eventually opening up to the serene, mirrored views of Lake Hawea and Wanaka.

  4. The Northy North (North Island): Heading north from Auckland, this trip takes you into a subtropical paradise rich with maritime history and golden beaches. As you reach the Bay of Islands, you’ll find a playground of 144 islands perfect for sailing and spotting dolphins, alongside the historic Waitangi Treaty Grounds where New Zealand’s founding document was signed. The journey continues to the very tip of the country at Cape Reinga, where you can watch the swirling meeting point of the Pacific Ocean and the Tasman Sea. On the return loop, the road winds through the Hokianga Harbour and the Waipoua Forest, home to Tāne Mahuta, a massive Kauri tree that has stood for over 2,000 years, offering a spiritual and ancient conclusion to the loop.

  5. The Southern Scenic Route (Deep South Island): For those seeking solitude and raw natural beauty, this route begins in the Scottish-influenced city of Dunedin and follows the wild southern coastline. You’ll wind through The Catlins, a region of hidden waterfalls, petrified forests, and windswept cliffs where rare yellow-eyed penguins and sea lions are often spotted. After passing through the southernmost city of Invercargill, the road turns north toward the towering granite peaks of Fiordland. The grand finale is the drive from Te Anau to Milford Sound, a road carved through solid rock and hanging valleys that leads to the breathtaking fiord itself, where waterfalls tumble hundreds of meters down sheer cliffs into the dark, still water below.
